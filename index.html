Learn Swahili for Black Power. In One Year.

Welcome to Kamit Swahili. It's the first African-Centered Swahili course available online, made by African people for African people. It's designed to help you get proficient in Swahili in one year.

How? By using an effective combination of practice-driven self-study modules and live weekly Q&A sessions with Swahili teachers from Kenya and Tanzania.

Why Swahili?

With almost 150 million people speaking it as their 1st or 2nd language, Swahili is the 7th most spoken language in the world. It's an authentic Bantu language that's spoken in the Central African Republic, the Comoros Islands, Malawi, Mozambique, and Northern Zambia.

It's an official language in Tanzania 🇹🇿, Kenya 🇰🇪, Rwanda 🇷🇼, Uganda 🇺🇬 and the Democratic Republic of Congo 🇨🇩.

If we want it, it'll be the common language for the United States of Africa that we're building for all Africans at home and abroad. 💪🏿 💪🏾 💪🏿

How Does The Course Work?

1. Self-study modules

Learn Swahili with a practice-driven approach. Each lesson teaches you a fundamental Swahili grammar concept and then focuses on your listening, speaking, reading and writing skills.

The content is interactive, fun and African-Centered, ideal to keep you motivated. Brief text explanations, video screencasts, quizzes and multimedia pronunciation exercices.

2. Weekly Q&A

Every week, we all get on Zoom to interact with our Swahili teachers. It's a great opportunity to practice your conversation skills with a native speaker and ask any questions.

All Q&A sessions are recorded. When you can't attend live, you're still able to watch the recording at your own time.

3. Private Community

Get access to our private online community where you can connect with brothers and sisters who have the same mindset and are as committed as you.

It's a great way to meet people, ask questions about Swahili or Pan African projects and issues. Our Swahili teachers are also there to help.

What's In The Course?

Three key modules that will take you from beginner to proficient.

Foundations Module 01. Naming Things

In Swahili there are no masculine and feminine genders. Sounds like good news but there are actually a lot more genders (or classes) than in English, depending of the noun. For example kiti (= chair) belongs to a different gender or class than mke (= woman).

The noun class system is the foundation of the Swahili language and the thing that causes the most difficulty to people learning Swahili. In this module of 8 lessons, we'll teach you an intuitive way to grasp how naming things work in Swahili. Then we'll help you master it with engaging interactive exercices.

Foundations Module 02. Describing Things

Once you know how to properly name things, it's time to be able to describe them. In Swahili, the adjectives and different pronouns change depending on the class of the noun they are referring to.

Throughout the 13 lessons of this module, you'll learn to use the different types of adjectives and pronouns, the date and time system, how to ask questions and compare things. Thanks to the fun interactive exercices, you'll soon be able to describe things confidently.

Foundations Module 03. Making sentences

In this last key module, you'll reach proficiency by learning to produce various types of sentences, using all the tenses and all the main grammatical constructs of the Swahili language.

With its 37 lessons, this module is the biggest but it's not more difficult than the previous ones. At the end you'll know more than 2000 words. You'll be able to understand the gist of most spoken conversations and written text in Swahili. You'll also be able to express yourself by speaking or writing. You'll be proficient.

Conversation Modules. Becoming Fluent

Once you finish the Foundations cycle, you can stay with us to gradually reach fluency level. The conversation modules are a form of virtual immersion in the Swahili world. The goal is to broaden your vocabulary, increase your listening, speaking, writing, and reading skills to make you an advanced Swahili speaker.

It's based upon a weekly personal study of a piece of media or a specific theme (news articles, essays, books, tales, news reports, tv shows, movies/documentaries, speeches, interviews, songs etc.). The piece is then discussed collectively during a live meeting & QA session on Zoom, involving our Swahili teachers and the other students at this level.
